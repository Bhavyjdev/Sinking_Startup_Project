Key Insights

  • Premium Users (28%): Most profitable segment with highest retention rate (82%) but expects premium service experience.
  • Budget Users (42%): Largest segment with moderate retention (61%) - highly sensitive to service costs and wait times.
  • Urban vs Rural Split: Urban users have 41% higher satisfaction with service quality but rural users represent critical growth opportunity.
  • Service Visit Frequency: Rural users have 62% more service visits annually, indicating potential product reliability issues.
  • Retention Drivers: Service quality impacts retention 2.4x more than product features for all segments except premium users.

Root Cause Analysis

Identifying the underlying factors driving customer experience issues through comprehensive causal analysis.

Service Center Operations

Current service centers operate on legacy ICE vehicle processes, creating inefficiencies when diagnosing and servicing electric vehicles. Technician expertise primarily oriented to conventional vehicles.

Primary

Supply Chain Management

Decentralized inventory system without real-time tracking creates parts availability issues. Average parts procurement time of 4.1 days vs industry standard of 1.5 days.

Primary

Staff Training & Expertise

Technical staff receive only 12 hours of EV-specific training compared to industry standard of 80+ hours. Knowledge gaps in EV diagnostics and troubleshooting.

Secondary

Communication Systems

No centralized customer communication platform. Updates provided manually by service advisors with 32% accuracy in estimated completion times.

Secondary

Quality Control

Limited pre-delivery and post-service quality checks. Only 17% of service centers perform comprehensive diagnostic scans post-repair.

Tertiary
